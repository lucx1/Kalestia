I. General sentence construction

1. How is a sentence constructed in Kalestia?

Usually every sentence follows this rule:

Subject

Predicate

Any other objects…

However, if you want to emphasize a specific object, you can move it to the beginning of a sentence, just like that:

Emphasized object

Subject

Predicate

Any other objects…

Yellow shadowed parts are required in a sentence. Green shadowed ones are optional. This means, a subject is not always needed if the context makes it clear who or what is the subject. It is again always possible to emphasize a subject by using the personal pronoun (a, o, e, ia, io, ie). Here are some examples:

Elf ifsnēse oliātan. = The elf likes the evening.

Oliātan elf ifsnēse. = The elf likes the evening. (The evening is emphasized here)

Ifsnēse oliātan. = He likes the evening.

Ifsnēsa oliātan. = I like the evening.

A ifsnēsa oliātan. = I like the evening. (I is emphasized here)

One additional thing to mention is that Kalestia does not have any articles like “the” or “a/an”.

IV. Grammar in use

1. Modal verbs

Words, which have the meaning of our modal verbs, does not have special grammar rules in Kalestia. Those constructions work with a gerund like it would be with any other verb (not with an infinitive, as it is the case in most of our languages).

Thus, the verbs such a modal verb refer to can get “-san”, “-sen”, “-sin”, “-son” or “-sun” added to the stem. In most cases, the vocal depends on the last vocal of the stem. Here are some examples:

a. Ability

he can eat - tīnse ferisin
he can write - tīnse milunsun

he can't eat - niu tīnse ferisin
he can't write - niu tīnse milunsun

b. Obligation

he must eat - numitse ferisin
he must write - numitse milunsun

he doesn't have to eat - niu numitse ferisin
he doesn't have to write - niu numitse milunsun

he must not eat - numitse niu ferisin
he must not write - numitse niu milunsun

he should eat - prizīse ferisin
he should write - prizīse milunsun

he shouldn't eat - prizīse niu ferisin
he shouldn't write - prizīse niu milunsun

c. Possibility/Assumption

he may be eating - lirāmse ferisin
he may be writing - lirāmse milunsun

he may not be eating - lirāmse niu ferisin
he may not be writing - lirāmse niu milunsun

he might be eating - misiāse ferisin
he might be writing - misiāse milunsun

he might not be eating - misiāse niu ferisin
he might not be writing - misiāse niu milunsun

he should be eating - atise ferisin
he should be writing - atise milunsun

he must be eating - eziāse ferisin
he must be writing - eziāse milunsun

d. Wish

he wants to eat - dizēse ferisin
he wants to write - dizēse milunsun

he doesn't want to eat - niu dizēse ferisin
he doesn't want to write - niu dizēse milunsun
