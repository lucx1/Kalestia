I. General sentence construction

1. How is a sentence constructed in Kalestia?

Usually every sentence follows this rule:

Subject

Predicate

Any other objects…

However, if you want to emphasize a specific object, you can move it to the beginning of a sentence, just like that:

Emphasized object

Subject

Predicate

Any other objects…

Yellow shadowed parts are required in a sentence. Green shadowed ones are optional. This means, a subject is not always needed if the context makes it clear who or what is the subject. It is again always possible to emphasize a subject by using the personal pronoun (a, o, e, ia, io, ie). Here are some examples:

Elf ifsnese oliatan. = The elf likes the evening.

Oliatan elf ifsnese. = The elf likes the evening. (The evening is emphasized here)

Ifsnese oliatan. = He likes the evening.

Ifsnesa oliatan. = I like the evening.

A ifsnesa oliatan. = I like the evening. (I is emphasized here)

One additional thing to mention is that Kalestia does not have any articles like “the” or “a/an”.

7. Possessive pronouns, adjectives and adverbs

Constrcting adjectives and possessive pronouns always follows the same rule.

Adjective stem OR Genitive of the personal pronoun

+

Suffix of the referring noun

In order to make an adjective or possessive pronoun an adverb, only add -ile to its stem.

IV. Grammar in use

1. Modal verbs

Words, which have the meaning of our modal verbs, does not have special grammar rules in Kalestia. Those constructions work with a gerund like it would be with any other verb (not with an infinitive, as it is the case in most of our languages).

Thus, the verbs such a modal verb refer to can get “-san”, “-sen”, “-sin”, “-son” or “-sun” added to the stem. In most cases, the vocal depends on the last vocal of the stem. Here are some examples:

a. Ability

he can eat - tinse ferisin
he can write - tinse milunsun

he can't eat - niu tinse ferisin
he can't write - niu tinse milunsun

b. Obligation

he must eat - numitse ferisin
he must write - numitse milunsun

he doesn't have to eat - niu numitse ferisin
he doesn't have to write - niu numitse milunsun

he must not eat - numitse niu ferisin
he must not write - numitse niu milunsun

he should eat - prizise ferisin
he should write - prizise milunsun

he shouldn't eat - prizise niu ferisin
he shouldn't write - prizise niu milunsun

c. Possibility/Assumption

he may be eating - liramse ferisin
he may be writing - liramse milunsun

he may not be eating - liramse niu ferisin
he may not be writing - liramse niu milunsun

he might be eating - misiase ferisin
he might be writing - misiase milunsun

he might not be eating - misiase niu ferisin
he might not be writing - misiase niu milunsun

he should be eating - atise ferisin
he should be writing - atise milunsun

he must be eating - eziase ferisin
he must be writing - eziase milunsun

d. Wish

he wants to eat - dizese ferisin
he wants to write - dizese milunsun

he doesn't want to eat - niu dizese ferisin
he doesn't want to write - niu dizese milunsun
